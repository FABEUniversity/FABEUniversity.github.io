Types of Courses

Curriculum at FABE for all Study Programs

At our university, we offer a diverse range of courses designed to equip students with essential skills and knowledge for the modern world. Our curriculum is divided into three distinct types of courses: foundational courses, project-based courses, and discussions. Each course type plays a crucial role in preparing students for the challenges and opportunities of the 21st century.

Building Core Competencies
Solving Real-Life Problems
Ethical and Social Foundations

  • Foundational Courses: Building Core Competencies

    Our foundational courses focus on developing core competencies that extend beyond traditional literacy and numeracy. These courses are designed to equip students with vital skills such as: -Data Literacy: Students learn to navigate, interpret, and communicate statistical information, both in personal and corporate settings. They also learn to curate, cite, and critically reflect on data sources. -Reading Competence: Students develop the ability to find, read, interpret, understand, and communicate about digital texts from diverse sources. Through these core competencies, students practice and learn how to effectively engage with and analyze information, laying a strong foundation for their academic and professional success.

  • Project-Based Courses: Solving Real-Life Problems

    Our project-based courses are implemented in collaboration with business partners who present real-life problems within the specific domain of a student's study. In these courses, students: - Develop cognitive skills such as critical and creative thinking, problem-solving, and decision-making. - Enhance metacognitive skills, allowing them to reflect on and critically analyze their own thinking processes, thereby "learning how to learn." - Build social skills through collaboration with teammates, university staff, and business partners. This includes communication, teamwork, and conflict resolution skills. - Acquire and apply physical and practical skills by manipulating materials and tools to achieve outcomes with new technologies. By tackling real-world challenges, students gain hands-on experience and practical knowledge, preparing them for successful careers in their chosen fields.

  • Discussions: Ethical and Social Foundations

    Our discussion-based courses encourage students to question existing standards, engage in ethical debates, and build strong social and emotional foundations. In these courses, students: - Engage in meaningful conversations about moral and ethical issues. - Develop social and emotional skills, including empathy and ethical reasoning. - Learn to articulate their thoughts clearly and consider different perspectives. Through these discussions, students not only broaden their understanding of complex social issues but also build the emotional intelligence needed to navigate the ethical challenges they will encounter in their personal and professional lives.

Experience Report

Day in a Life of a Student at FABE University

This is Leo, a second-year student at FABE University, who is pursuing an interdisciplinary major that combines environmental science and artificial intelligence. Driven by a passion for sustainable technologies and innovative solutions, Leo thrives in FABE's dynamic learning environment.

8:00 AM - Personalized Study Session

The day begins with a personalized study session at home. Using his AI-Tutor AITLE , Leo logs in to review his tailored study plan. The AI-driven system provides a mix of interactive modules, video lectures, and reading materials that cater to his learning style and progress. Based on these information the AI-tutor AITLE generates personalized study sessions for Leo. It also analyses his performance and suggests targeted exercises to strengthen weak areas.

10:00 AM - Hybrid Class on Sustainable Engineering

Leo heads to campus for a hybrid class. The course is part of a collaboration with business partners from the energy sector, designed to tackle real-world problems. Today’s session includes both in-person project work and virtual participation from industry experts working in renewable energy.
The students are working on a project to develop innovative solutions for sustainable energy management. During the seminar, they wonder when the registration for the project work begins, and their AI tutor answers this question.

12:00 PM - Wellness Activity

After lunch break Leo participates in a yoga session via FABEs mental health course programs. These sessions are designed to promote mental and physical health, ensuring students maintain a balanced lifestyle.

2:00 PM - Virtual Seminar on Digital Skills

Leo attends a virtual seminar, facilitated through FOLT. Students here gain insights into digital tools that are useful for everyday professional and academic life. Today, the focus is on the mindful use of chatbots in seminar papers. Some professors are also participating in the course.

3:00 PM - Lab Session on Green Technologies

Back on campus, Leo participates in a lab session focusing on green technologies. This session is part of a course that combines chemical engineering, environmental policy, and business strategy. Using advanced lab equipment and simulations, students experiment with new materials for solar panels, considering both the scientific and economic aspects of their innovations.

5:00 PM - Online Seminar on Ethics in AI

Leo joins a seminar on ethics in AI, a critical component of their education at FABE. The seminar, accessible via FOLT, features speakers from diverse fields, including philosophy, computer science, and law. Discussions revolve around the ethical implications of AI in society, encouraging students to think critically about their future roles as innovators.

7:00 PM - Team Collaboration on MOOC

Leo collaborates with his project team via a MOOC (Massive Open Online Course) platform. Using tools like Teams, Miro, and Slack, they discuss their ongoing project: developing a sustainable urban infrastructure model. Each team member, including international peers, shares updates and insights, facilitated by the seamless integration of these tools into FOLT.

8:00 PM - Personalized Help and Study Review

As the day winds down the AI tutor helps Leo create his study plan. For his modules, the AI creates schedules for when he should study, when assignments are due, and what else is coming up in his studies. Next semester, for example, Leo has an internship. The tutor suggests companies that might fit his study profile and helps him write an application letter.
